AGENDA & SESSIONS

KEYNOTE:

9:00 am PT: 5 Strategies for Building a Culture of Everyday, Everywhere Experimentation

In a world of exponential change, catching the future depends less on planning for what will come next and more on continuously experimenting with what could come next. To stay relevant, an organization has to invest preemptively in developing alternatives to the status quo—by testing new technologies, exploring new ways of going to market, engaging new sorts of customers, and plotting out new business models. It has to be constantly generating new strategic options. In other words, in order to innovate and adapt in this creative, disruptive economy, every organization must develop the capacity (and the appetite) for experimentation. In this fast-paced presentation—full of rich stories and radically practical perspectives and practices—Polly LaBarre will share five strategies for building a culture of everyday, everywhere, every person experimentation.

Experimentation Center of Excellence

9:50 am PT: Experimenting for Maximum Business Impact

Testing is great, learning is better. If you let it, uninspired testing can put your company to sleep. If your testing focuses on small scale page elements like button color, you could be stifling vital creative thinking, and miss the big picture. What you want is to foster strategic experimentation throughout the business. In this session, you will hear how Hotwire uses experimentation to learn about customer needs, capture signal and test into the next big opportunity.

10:30 am PT: Building an Experimentation Center of Excellence

Experimentation has grown beyond being the domain of growth hackers to become firmly embedded in international, cross-disciplinary teams and workflows. This evolution has created no shortage of challenges and opportunities for the companies willing to tackle them head on. In this session learn how to scalably address the challenges and opportunities that experimentation creates with a world-class center of excellence.

11:10 am PT: Interpreting Results with Multiple Goals, Variations, and Segments. Oh my!

As your experimentation program becomes more sophisticated, a simple a/b test composed of 1 variation, 1 baseline and 1 goal no longer supports the goals of your organization. In this session Leo Pekelis, Optimizely’s chief statistician, explains how to interpret results from experiments that use multiple goals, variations and segments using real examples and shares how Optimizely’s Stats Engine protects you from the pitfalls of running multiple comparisons on an experiment.

Personalization

9:50 am PT: Driving the Ultimate Customer Experience Through Personalization

In this session, attendees will walk through the opportunities and challenges of personalization - from 'one-to-most' to the elusive 'one-to-one' experiences. Presenter Reid Bryant will explain how to algorithmically find the most differentiated and impactful customer segments through cluster analysis, the benefits of a tailored user experience, how to target segments through your testing tools, and how to quantify the impact to revenue.

10:30 am PT: Recommendations Reboot: How to Continuously Improve your Recommendations ROI

Recommendations are table stakes for modern organizations, but by now it’s no secret that the effort required to both manually select recommended products and experiment with styles and layouts is preventing retailers from maximizing potential revenue. In this \ session learn how leading retailer, Stella & Dot, is applying an experimentation mindset to their recommendations strategy and the benefits they have reaped thus far. Also, get a sneak peek at how Optimizely is implementing recommended content for its blog and Knowledge Base.

11:10 am PT: Optimizely on Optimizely, our ABM Journey

How do you build consensus with sales and then implement account based marketing programs you can trust to deepen engagement with the accounts that matter most? Join this session to learn from Optimizely’s ABM playbook. Hear from Steph Robotham, Optimizely’s VP of Corporate Marketing and Heather Watkins, Optimizely’s Dir. of Marketing Programs, on how to transform your organization, your programs and successfully generate quality sales pipeline in your target accounts, verticals and geographies.

Experiment Everywhere

9:50 am PT: Experimentation Across the Tech Stack

Web applications are essential to building lasting customer relationships and maximizing the impact of your development team’s efforts. In this session General Assembly and Tripping.com will share how they think about enabling their technical teams to experiment deeply and broadly across their technology stack. Attend this session and get real-life use cases for server side testing, insights into enabling your technical teams and advice for getting started and driving adoption.

10:30 am PT: Retain or Die: The Retention Playbook

Retention is critical to the survival of every business. You can spend time and money acquiring users, but without a solid retention strategy, you’re throwing that money away. The retention playbook, tested with examples from growth-stage companies, will provide you with a framework for diagnosing and improving your retention at all stages of the customer lifecycle.

11:10 am PT: The Path to Mobile-Only: What and Where to Optimize Your Experience for Today's Consumer

Phones drive more shopping traffic than any other device -- proving that consumers are already mobile-first. As we prepare for the next milestone - a mobile-only shopping world - retailers must focus their investments on optimizing the experience for mobile shoppers. In this session, we'll reveal the current state of mobile shopping, share a series of real-world experiments from leading retailers and provide an approach to designing an effective mobile testing program.

Plenary Closing Session

11:50 - 12:20 pm: Leveraging Psychological Principles for Building Better Digital Experiences

As a digital professional you are constantly seeking to meet the needs of your online audience by building customer empathy and understanding visitor data. This session will explore the connections between human emotion, cognition and attention as the basis for how users interact with web and mobile experiences, and how to leverage these psychological principles to build enhanced digital experiences.
